Biology.

The human body possesses a well-documented capacity called cardiac entrainment — the tendency of heart rate to synchronize with rhythmic acoustic stimuli. When exposed to a steady, slowing beat, the autonomic nervous system shifts from sympathetic dominance to parasympathetic recovery.

This isn't relaxation through association. It's a direct physiological pathway. Studies using polysomnography confirm that auditory entrainment increases sleep spindle density and reduces sleep onset latency by an average of 11 minutes — without pharmacological intervention.

SlowBeat's 30-minute descent protocol mirrors the natural cardiac deceleration observed in healthy adult sleep initiation, mapped from aggregated polysomnography data across clinical sleep studies conducted between 2008–2021.
